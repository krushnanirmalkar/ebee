The Challenges Holding Properties Back From EV Readiness
  • High Infrastructure Cost
  • Limited Electrical Capacity
  • Future Expansion
  • Government Compliance
  • Intelligent Power Distribution
  • Operational Complexity

High Infrastructure Cost

Traditional EV charging deployments require oversized transformers and expensive electrical upgrades long before the first charger is installed.

Common across commercial developments, hotels, shopping malls and residential builders.

Limited Electrical Capacity

Most existing properties were never designed for widespread EV charging, making local grid capacity the ultimate bottleneck.

Common across housing societies, hotels, office campuses and commercial buildings.

Future Expansion

Installing infrastructure for today's EV demand leaves properties unable to scale as adoption inevitably grows.

Common across residential projects, commercial projects and mixed-use developments.

Government Compliance

Evolving building codes mandate EV-ready parking, forcing developers to meet strict regulations while managing spiraling project costs.

Common across commercial developers, residential builders and township projects.

Intelligent Power Distribution

Supplying power efficiently across multiple charging points requires active load management to avoid overloading the property's grid.

Critical for all property types transitioning to electric mobility.

Operational Complexity

Managing multiple hardware chargers, resident utility billing, and ongoing maintenance through fragmented tools creates operational overhead.

A challenge faced by facility managers across all property classes.
